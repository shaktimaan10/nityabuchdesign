Player Jersey Swap

A quite popular image manipulation technique using Adobe Photoshop. I edit almost all Jersey Swaps on players using 2 editing methods.

The first method is used for swapping monochrome jerseys and kits with the same patterns including stripes and prints. I select the jersey, and change the color of the kit to the color of the new kit. Next step is to remove the Club badges, sponsor logos and other minute details from the kit and position the same assets of the new jersey at the perfect place.

The other method is helpful when I need to swap kits with different patterns and designs. In this case, a new layer of jersey is pasted on the top of the player image. All that is needed is a photograph of any player in a similar stance wearing the required jersey. I cut out the jersey from one image and place on the other, make a few positioning adjustments and kits can be swapped.

There are other methods to doing it, however these 2 methods get the job done most of the time.
